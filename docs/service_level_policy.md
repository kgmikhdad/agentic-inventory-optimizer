# Service Level Policy

High-priority products should target approximately 98% service level. These include critical medicines, essential grocery items, and high-importance spare parts.

Medium-priority products should target approximately 95% service level. These include regular selling products with moderate margins and stable customer demand.

Low-priority products should target approximately 90% service level. These include accessories, slow-moving items, and non-critical products where holding cost is more important than immediate availability.

When budget is constrained, high-priority products should receive reorder allocation before low-priority products if the stockout risk is similar.
