# Inventory Policy

Products should be reordered when current stock falls below the reorder point. The reorder point is calculated as expected demand during supplier lead time plus safety stock.

Safety stock should increase when demand uncertainty is high, when supplier lead time is long, or when the product has high business priority.

The inventory manager should avoid ordering quantities far above the 30-day forecast unless the product has long shelf life, high service-level requirement, or supplier minimum order quantity constraints.

Products with high stockout risk should be prioritized when budget is limited. Overstocked products should not be reordered unless there is a contractual or supplier-specific reason.
